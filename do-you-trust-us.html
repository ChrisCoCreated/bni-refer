The invite

Imagine being introduced to a group of local business people who meet regularly to help one another, share referrals, and grow their businesses together. That is the invitation here: not a pitch to join on the spot, just a chance to visit and see whether it feels right.

If you have tried networking before and felt underwhelmed, that is fair. Different groups work in different ways. The point of a visit is to show you what this chapter is actually like before you make any decisions.

How the chapter works

  • An online chapter based around Canterbury and the surrounding area.
  • Meetings on Zoom, which keeps the commute out of the way and the commitment easier to manage.
  • 60-second introductions and a short featured presentation are still part of the meeting.
  • Because the chapter is online, members can join from home, the office, a car, or anywhere with a decent connection.
  • Lower overheads mean the experience can be cheaper than a traditional breakfast meeting.

Why it is worth a look

Even if you decide it is not for you, a visit still exposes you to new people, new opportunities, and a clearer picture of how referral networking works in practice.

You might leave with a handful of useful ideas, a better sense of local businesses, and a clearer answer to whether this kind of group suits your style.

The payoff

What do you get from one visit?

A single meeting can be enough to see whether the group feels collaborative, useful, and worth coming back to. That is the whole point of the invitation.

Practical exposure

Free, face-to-face exposure to a wider network of businesses, services, and referral opportunities.

A lighter Friday

No room hire, no breakfast run, and no long drive. Just a focused early meeting that leaves the rest of the day open.

A clearer decision

If the format is not right for you, you still leave with context. If it is right, you already know the next step.
