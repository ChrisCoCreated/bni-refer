# Thrive Homecare Brand Guidelines Reference

Reference summary for BNI event pages and other Thrive-branded touchpoints.

## About Thrive Homecare

- Dedicated care and wellbeing provider.
- Tailored approach with meticulous attention to detail and personalised support.
- Companionship and care designed to bring joy and meaning to everyday life.
- Mission: help people enjoy the best possible later life.
- The brand speaks to people supported and their families for as long as they need it.

## Core Values

- Empowering: enabling older people, families, and team members to thrive.
- Deeply Connected: building strong relationships with clients and the local community.
- Impactful: making a positive difference to quality of life.
- Creative: taking an individualised approach to care and wellbeing.

## Tone of Voice

- Friendly and approachable.
- Confident, never arrogant.
- Expert but accessible.
- Professional but warm.
- Progressive but grounded.
- Empathetic but focused.

## Colour Palette

- Purple: `#6d3ac6`
- Pink: `#ce3f8f`
- Yellow: `#ffd23f`
- Blue: `#1db9d2`
- Green: `#3aceac`

## Colour Usage

- Yellow is the primary colour and should be used as a background colour.
- Use the other palette colours as accents and supporting elements.
- Most text should be pure black or pure white.
- Keep layouts bright, warm, and easy to read.

## Typography

- Font family: `Onest`
- Use Onest Black for headings.
- Use Onest Regular for body copy.
- Keep typography clean, modern, and highly legible.

## Tagline Usage

- The phrase `Live Life in Colour` can be styled by colouring the word `colour` with brand colours when it appears in approved marketing copy.
- Do not add the tagline to page headers unless it is already part of the intended copy.

## Event Page Guidance

- Header label: `BNI Members Event`
- Keep the page aligned with Thrive Homecare branding, not generic BNI styling.
- Prioritise clarity for RSVP, calendar download, and email draft actions.
